Background

Emrich took his first coding course in his junior year of highschool. That sparked a passion to pursue Computer Science at Syracuse University and Computer Systems at City Tech. With a declining passion for STEM Emrich took a step away from coding but found that passion again during the pandemic with the FlatIron School. Besides spending countless hours learning Full Stack Web Development, Emrich has also spent his time building his powerlifting coaching business, where he has developed a skill to build strong client relationships with people. He loves to work and help people. Besides engineering Emrich loves to compete in powerlifting, play with his dog Kilo and explore the city. New York City born and raised, Emrich has just relocated to Los Angeles California and is now looking to find work in Software Development in the LA area or work remotely.



Career

The Story

After I graduated from the FlatIron School in June 2021, I moved from New York City to Los Angeles and began my job hunt. I spent months interviewing with companies and kept getting to the later rounds of the interview process and it was frustrating. The search for that first position in software is tough. It's a never ending battle of cold outreach's, people who don't follow up when they said they would, and No's. However, I learned from my time at The FlatIron School that consistency and determination is key (corny I know, but it's true!). But after months of rejection I was finally offered a 3 month trial internship with Tango Analytics as a Software Developer UI Intern. I then spent the next 3 months learning a ton and I can split this up into 2 categories, technical and working in corporate. I added VueJS (2.0 and 3.0) and Quasar to my front-end framework toolkit. I also increased my skills as a debugger ten-fold. I was shown many tricks and methods to going about debugging the front-end map product and now have a much deeper understanding of how to use the chrome developer console to my advantage. On the corporate side of things, I learned all about how teams can operate and work together. I learned to always be in communication with my superiors and keep them updated/ask them for help to stay efficient. I was able to finally get a taste of what it was like to be a software engineer. After the trial was over, I received a return offer from Tango Analytics to be a Full-Time Salaried Jr. Software Developer. Since then the team has grown and I have continuted to hone the skills mentioned above and more!

The Technical

So I figured that whomever is reading this might also be interested in hearing what I've learned so far and the more technical things I've done since starting my software career. So I wanted to outline that here in a way where I'm not breaking my NDA. I first started out by self teaching myself Vue2, Vue3 and Quasar. This is because I entered the company just as they were trying to migrate the entire platform to the newest version of the front-end frameworks we use. So I have spent my time since then taking components from the older version of the code and changing them to the newest version syntax and then debugging from there to make sure the components acted the same way. I spent much of my time debugging and I think I've actually grown to enjoy it. I learned how to use breakpoints and check the network tab to see how and when data is being called. I would also interact with the dev console in breakpoints to check the values of certain variables to make sure the code was functional. We are now just getting to making reported bug fixes and are mostly finished with the migration project!

What Now?

Currently, I am very happy with my current position, however I am now putting out feelers again. I would like to continue to work on myself as a software engineer and desire a role with more collaboration and more creation. Even though I do love debugging I miss the creating part of software engineering. I miss making a whole app or even adding features. I would love to do more of that in my next role because I feel like that is when I enjoy software engineering the most. I love making things, however I still absolutely love the feeling of solving a bug and would love for my next role to include problem solving in that sense too!

WorkoutonRails



WorkoutonRails is a full Ruby on Rails web app, built using the MVC pattern. It includes the use of the OmniAuth gem that allows you to create an account using google credentials. WorkoutonRails lets a user create, find and manage their workouts! Users can comment and view others workouts too! It also uses Sqlite3 and ActiveRecord to design a fast and organized database.
